1 Advanced Geometry Features {#advanced_geometry}
2 ============
3 This tutorial will highlight many of the advanced geometric modeling capabilities available in PGSuper. These capabilities include:
4 
5 * Complex roadway geometry including multiple horizontal curves with transition spirals
6 * Complex alignment profiles including multiple, non-symmetric vertical curves
7 * Complex superelevation transitions including multiple crown point offsets
8 * Flared (non-parallel) girders
9 * Tapered deck edges with spline transitions
10 * Different girder spacing at each end of a span
11 * Different number of girders in each span
12 * Different girder spacing on either side of a pier
13 * Different types of girders within a bridge
14 
15 Example Bridge 1
16 -----------------
17 The bridge we will be modeling has 3 - 80ft spans.
18 * Abutment 1 is located at station 1+20.
19 * Span 1 has 5-WF58G girders at 6'-0\".
20 * Span 2 has 4-WF58G girders spaced at 8'-0\" at the centerline of pier 2 and 6'-0\" at the centerline of pier 3.
21 * Span 3 has 4-WF58G girders spaced at 6'-0\".
22 
23 The bridge deck is 31 ft (16' on left and 15' on right of CL bridge) wide at pier 2 and 24.5 ft (12.5' on left and 12' on right of CL bridge) wide at pier 3. The deck is a constant width, and parallels the alignment between Abutment 1 and Pier 2 and also between Pier 3 and Abutment 4. The deck edge is a spline taper between pier 2 and 3.
24 
25 The bridge is on a curved portion of the alignment. The back tangent is N 90 E, the PI station is 5+00 with a delta of 25 L and Radius of 2000 ft.
