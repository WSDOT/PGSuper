1 Capacity/Demand Ratios {#tg_capacity_demand_ratios}
2 ======================================
3 Capacity/Demand (C/D) ratios are reported in several places in PGSuper (examples are stresses, moment capacity,...) The rules for reporting the ratios are consistent throughout, and are as follows:
4 
5 * C/D is only computed when the algebraic signs of Capacity and Demand are the same
6 * C/D's are reported using two decimal places in the form "(2.22)"
7 * When C/D exceeds 10.0, it is reported as "(10+)"
8 * When Demand exceeds Capacity the specification check is always reported as FAIL. A tolerance is never applied to make C/D equal 1.0.
9 * For the case of zero capacity (e.g., zero allowable tension) C/D is not reported.
10 * When Demand is 0.0 and Capacity is non-zero, C/D is reported as infinity.
11 * For the case of sign reversals, C/D is reported as "(-)". For example, C is positive moment capacity and D is a negative moment.
