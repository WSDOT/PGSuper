Girder Description - Strand Extensions/Debonding

This tab is used to define extended straight strands and debonding of straight strands in the girder. The name of this tab will be "Strand Extensions" when only strands can be extended (no debonding). The name will be "Debonding" if any strands can be debonded.

Debonded strands are shown in red in the picture and in the Girder View. This tab shows the location and numbering of actual strands.

Describing Debonded Strands

  1. Click on the check box in the Debond column to designate a strand as debonded. If a strand cannot be debonded, there will not be a check box.

  2. Define the distance(s) from the end of the girder to the debond location by typing it into the Debond Length column of the grid.

  3. Check the Symmetrical Debonding box to make the debond length the same at both ends of the girder

Debond lengths cannot exceed one-half of the girder length.

Note: Straight strands that are shown in grey cannot be debonded. This happens when the strand is not allowed to be debonded in the girder library entry.

Describing Extended Strands

  1. Strands that are not debonded may be designated as extended by checking the box in the Extend Left and Extend Right columns.

Note: Extending Strands is only available if the Allow Strand Extensions options is enabled in the Project Criteria on the Casting Yard tab.

Note: Extended strands are considered to be 100% developed along their entire length at the Strength limit states.
