Girder Description - Debonding/Strand Extensions

This tab is used to define debonding and extensions of straight strands. The tab is titled Debonding when strands can be debonded, otherwise it is titled Strand Extensions.

Debonded strands are shown in red and Extended strands are shown in blue in the schematic picture and in the Girder View.

Defining Debonded and Extended Strands

To define a debonded strand, check the box in the debond column and enter the distance from the end of the girder to the point where the strand is bonded in the Debond Length column. If the Symmetrical Debonding option is checked the same debond length is used from both ends of the girder.

Debond lengths cannot exceed one-half of the girder length.

To define an extended strand, check the box in one or both of the Extend columns.

Debonded strands cannot be extended

Extended strands are considered to be fully developed over the entire lenght of the girder when computing moment capacity, shear capacity, and evaluating the longitudinal reinforcement for shear requirements.
