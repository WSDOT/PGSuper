Model a Special Dead Load

A 10 kip overhead sign structure is placed at mid-span (station 2+33.66). The entire sign weight is assumed to be split evenly between the exterior girders on either side of the bridge. Hence we need to apply a 5 kip point load at the mid-span of girder A. The load will be applied to the composite structure in Bridge Site Stage 2.

NOTE: PGSuper models the various steps in the sequence of bridge construction as stages. The stages are:
Casting Yard - Non-composite girder in the casting yard just after prestress transfer
Lifting - Girder is lifted from the casting bed
Shipping - Girder is being shipped to the bridge site
Girder Placement - The girder is placed on the bridge piers
Temporary Strand Removal - If temporary strands are used, this is when the are removed
Deck and Diaphragm Placement (Bridge Site Stage 1) - The concrete deck and diaphragms are cast onto the non-composite girder
Superimposed Dead Loads (Bridge Site Stage 2) - Superimposed dead loads (such as traffic barriers) are applied to the composite girder
Final with Live Load (Bridge Site Stage 3) - This is the final bridge configuration including live load

The girder view is the most convenient place to view and edit user-defined loads.

  1. Click on the icon on the tool bar to bring up the girder view.

  2. Select "Bridge Site 2" as the stage to view/edit loads in.

  3. Drag a point load to mid-span by clicking your left mouse button on the icon on the toolbar and dragging it to the middle of the girder in the view. When the girder is highlighted, drop the load by releasing the mouse button. The outcome should look something like the following:
