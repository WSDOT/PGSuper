Understanding Analysis Results

Before learning how to access the analysis results, we need to take a few minutes to understand how analysis results are categorized in PGSuper.

Stages

PGSuper categorizes analysis results based on the construction sequence of a typical precast girder structure. Precast girder structures are assembled in stages. Each stage depends upon the previous one. The analysis results are presented in terms of stage. The analysis stages are defined as:

Casting Yard

The girder is in the casting yard and the prestressing has just been imparted (released) into the girder.

Lifting/Handling

The girder is lifted from the casting bed and put into temporary storage while the concrete cures.

Shipping/Hauling

The girder is placed on a truck and shipped to the bridge site.

Girder Placement

The girder is at the bridge site and placed on its simple span supports.

Temporary Strand Removal

If the girder contains temporary strands, this is when the strands are removed. Strand removal changes the stress profile in the girder.

Deck and Diaphragm Placement
(Bridge Site Stage 1)

The girder is at the bridge site. The non-composite girder section is supporting the weight of the wet slab and diaphragms. It is assumed that all have losses occurred by this stage unless a refined method of loss prediction is used (refined losses from AASHTO LRFD Bridge Design Specification 3rd Edition with 2005 interim provision and later).

Superimposed Dead Load
(Bridge Site Stage 2)

The slab has cured and is composite with the girder. The composite girder section is supporting the weight of superimposed dead load including traffic barrier and overlay.

Final with Live Load
(Bridge Site Stage 3)

The bridge is open to traffic and carrying live load. The dead load of a future overlay is added in this stage.
