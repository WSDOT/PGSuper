Moment Capacity

Prior to the 2006 interim provisions to the AASHTO LRFD Bridge Design Specifications, WSDOT deviated from standard practice. Since 2006, AASHTO practice has come in line with WSDOT. Select the methodology for computing moment capacities and handling over-reinforced sections for the 2005 LRFD Specifications and earilier.

Mild steel longitudinal reinforcement in a girder can optionally be accounted for in ultimate moment capacity calculations. Refer to this section in the Theoretical Manual for more information.

The modulus of rupture can differ from AASHTO LRFD 5.4.2.6 by specifying the coefficient used in the computation for normal weight, sand lightweight, and all lightweight concrete.

The resistance factor is defined in AASHTO LRFD 5.5.4.2. The tension and compression controlled factors for normal and lightweight concrete can be given different values if needed.

Negative Moment Capacity

Deck longitudinal reinforcement provides all tensile section forces in the negative moment region of the structure. This option specifies whether non-composite loads are included when computing Mu for negative moment.
