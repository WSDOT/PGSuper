Temporary Strands Tab

Temporary strands are straight strands typically placed in the top flange of the girder to increase stability for handling and to reduce camber requirements. Temporary strands can be placed for one of two stagings:

  1. Pre-tensioned at casting time and cut in the field just prior to slab placement and after the diaphragms are cast. Or,

  2. Post-tensioned just after casting and cut in the field just prior to slab placement and after the diaphragms are cast.

Temporary strands are assumed to be bonded for 10 feet at either end of the girder and debonded along the remainder of the girder.

Strand Grid

Use the strand grid control to define the possible locations and fill order of temporary straight strands in the girder.

The strand location grid control allows you to add, modify, and delete template locations for strands in a girder section. Strand coordinates are directly input into the grid. The following rules apply:

  • The origin of the coordinate system for strand locations is the bottom center of the section as shown in the Figure above.

  • X values cannot be negative. If a non-zero X value is given, two strands are added for the fill location: one strand at (X,Y) and another at (-X,Y). This enforces the requirement that all strand templates must be symmetric about the Y axis.

  • Strands are added to the section in the given Fill # sequence.

  • Strand locations may be added above the currently-selected row by clicking the “Insert” button.

  • Strand locations may be appended the last row of the list by clicking the “Append” button.

  • Strand locations may be removed by first selecting the row and then clicking on the “Delete Row” button.

  • Strands must be located inside of the girder.
