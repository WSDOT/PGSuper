Hauling Tab

Use this tab to define criteria that is pertinent during hauling of the girder to the bridge site.

Note: All values on this tab are disabled if criteria checking for hauling is disable on the Design Tab

Analysis Method

PGSuper can perform a hauling analysis and design using either the method defined by the Washington State DOT (WSDOT)  or the Kansas DOT (KDOT). Note that the approaches for each method are very different - Refer to the Theoretical Manual for a description of both methods.

Washington State DOT Method (WSDOT)

The WSDOT method accounts for non-linear effects due to long girder instability. Input parameters for the WSDOT method are as follows:

WSDOT Parameters - Factors of Safety

Minimum Factor of Safety Against Cracking

Enter the minimum factor of safety to use against cracking of the section during hauling of the girders to the bridge site. This value is calculated using the method outlined in Robert F. Mast’s paper . The paper recommends a factor of safety of 1.0 for cracking, however, this value should be investigated by decision makers in your agency.

Minimum Factor of Safety Against Failure

Enter the minimum factor of safety to use against failure of the section during hauling of the girders to the bridge site. This value is calculated using the method outlined in Robert F. Mast’s paper. The paper recommends a factor of safety of 1.5 for failure, however, this value should be investigated by decision makers in your agency.

Modulus of Rupture for Cracking Moment

Enter the coefficient for computing the modulus of rupture used in computing the cracking moment.

Impact Factors

Enter the factor in percent that force effects are to be increased due to dynamic effects during hauling. The factor to be applied to the static load shall be taken as: (1 + IM/100). For example, a downward factor of 50.0 would increase vertical forces and stresses due to hauling by 50%. An upward factor of 30.0 would decrease vertical forces and stresses due to hauling by 30%. The factor must be between zero and 100.0.

WSDOT Parameters - Allowable Concrete Stresses

Compressive Stress

Enter the multiplier for the maximum allowable concrete compressive stress for hauling loads. Refer to Section 5.9.4.2.1 in the AASHTO LRFD Specifications for the recommended value.

Tensile Stress

Enter the multiplier for the maximum allowable concrete tensile stress for hauling loads. Refer to Section 5.9.4.2.2 in the AASHTO LRFD Specifications for the recommended value.

WSDOT Parameters - Hauling Parameters

Roll Stiffness of Truck Trailer

Enter the roll stiffness for truck trailers used to haul girders to the bridge site. This value is likely to be agency-specific, if not job-specific. Mast’s paper uses a value of 20,250 kip-in/radian, however your organization should perform a study to determine a value appropriate in your area.

Roll stiffness can be entered as a constant value or as a function of the girder weight. The heavier the girder, the more stiff and sturdy the truck will have to be. To define the roll stiffness as a function of the girder weight, enter the maximum weight per axle and the per axle stiffness. The roll stiffness will be determined by determining the number of axles required and multiplying that value by the per axle roll stiffness. A minimum roll stiffness is also required to represent the most common trucks used in your area.

Height of Girder Bottom Above Roadway

Enter the height of the bottom of the girder above the roadway for the standard truck for your agency.

Height of Roll Center of Truck Above Roadway

Enter the height of the roll center above the roadway for the standard truck for your agency.

Truck Width (C-C distance between dual tires)

Enter the width of the standard truck for your agency. The width of the truck is measured as the center to center distance between the left hand side and right hand side dual tires. (This parameter is 2Zmax)

Max Distance Between Girder Supports

Enter the maximum distance between girder support locations (trailer length) for the standard truck for your agency.

Max Expected Roadway Superelevation

Enter the maximum expected slope of the roadway elevation to be encountered by the vehicle hauling the girder to the bridge site. This is a unitless slope value, so a six percent slope would be entered as 0.06

Max Girder Sweep Tolerance

Enter the maximum girder sweep tolerance expected for manufacturers in your area. The sweep tolerance is the horizontal deviation of the girder from end to end along the total length of the girder. The Portland Cement Institute (PCI) recommends a sweep tolerance of 1/8inch per 10 feet = 0.00104.

Support Placement Lateral Tolerance

Enter the lateral tolerance for placement of girder onto support points.

Increase Girder C.G. Height for Camber by

Enter the percentage to increase the height of the C.G. of the girder above the roll center to account for camber. Mast’s paper uses a value of 2 percent, however your organization should perform a study to determine a value appropriate in your area.

Maximum Girder Weight

Enter the maximum girder weight. This value will depend on the plant lift capabilities of your local precasters and the capabilities of the available trucking, rail, and barge equipment. Your organization should perform a study to determine a value appropriate in your area. 

WSDOT Parameters - Design

Min Truck Support Location from ends of girder

Enter the minimum truck support location from the ends of the girder. The designer and the fabrication optimization analysis will use this as the starting point for the truck support locations. The keyword H may be entered to indicate the minimum location is H from the end of the girder.

Truck Support Location Accuracy

Enter the accuracy of the truck support locations. The designer and the fabrication optimization analysis will use this accuracy when locating the truck supports.

 

Kansas DOT Method (KDOT)

The KDOT method is a simplified approach that accounts for amplified girder stresses caused by dynamic effects of hauling. Non-linear effects due to long girder instability are not considered. Input parameters for the KDOT method are as follows:

KDOT Parameters - Dynamic Load Factors

Dynamic "g" factors are applied to girder dead loads to simulate dynamic effects at girder hauling. Typically for KDOT, a dynamic factor of 3.0 is applied along cantilevers, and a factor of 1.0 is applied between the cantilever sections. The dynamic affect is only applied downward.

"g" Factor Applied Along Cantilever

Enter the "g" factor to be applied to girder dead load along the cantilever (i.e., outside of bunk point support locations). This value must be greater than or equal to 1.0.

"g" Factor Applied Between Cantilevers

Enter the "g" factor to be applied to girder dead load between cantilevers (i.e., interior of beam between bunk point support locations). This value must be greater than or equal to 1.0.

KDOT Parameters - Allowable Concrete Stresses

Compressive Stress

Enter the multiplier for the maximum allowable concrete compressive stress for hauling loads. Refer to the KDOT LRFD Bridge Design Specifications for the recommended value.

Tensile Stress

Enter the multiplier for the maximum allowable concrete tensile stress for hauling loads. Refer to the KDOT LRFD Bridge Design Specifications for the recommended value.

KDOT Parameters - Support Location

Minimum Truck Support Location from ends of girder

Enter the minimum truck support location from the ends of the girder. This value is specified by an exact location, and by an optional fraction of girder length.

A warning will be reported if either value is exceeded.

The designer will use this as the starting point for design of truck support locations.

Truck Support Location Accuracy

Enter the accuracy of the truck support locations to be used by the design algorithm. The designer will use this accuracy when locating the truck supports.
