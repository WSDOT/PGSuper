Lifting Tab

Use this tab to define criteria for Lifting the girder in the Casting Yard

Factors of Safety

Minimum Factor of Safety Against Cracking

Enter the minimum factor of safety to use against cracking of the section during handling in the casting yard. This value is calculated using the method outlined in Robert F. Mast’s paper . The paper recommends a factor of safety of 1.0 for cracking, however, this value should be investigated by decision makers in your agency.

Minimum Factor of Safety Against Failure

Enter the minimum factor of safety to use against failure of the section during handling in the casting yard. This value is calculated using the method outlined in Robert F. Mast’s paper . The paper recommends a factor of safety of 1.5 for failure, however, this value should be investigated by decision makers in your agency.

Modulus of Rupture for Cracking Moment

Enter the coefficient for computing the modulus of rupture used in computing the cracking moment.

Impact Factors

Enter the factor that force effects are to be increased due to dynamic effects during lifting. For example, a downward factor of 0.5 would increase vertical forces and stresses due to hauling by 50%. An upward factor of 0.3 would decrease vertical forces and stresses due to hauling by 30%. The factor must be between zero and 1.0.

Allowable Concrete Stresses

Compressive Stress

Enter the multiplier for the maximum allowable concrete compressive stress for lifting loads in the casting yard. Refer to Section 5.9.4.2.1 in the AASHTO LRFD Specifications for the recommended value.

Tensile Stress

Enter the multiplier for the maximum allowable concrete tensile stress for lifting loads in the casting yard. Refer to Section 5.9.4.2.2 in the AASHTO LRFD Specifications for the recommended value.

Parameters - Lifting in Casting Yard

Height of Pick Point Above Top of Girder

Enter the height of the rigid pick point of the top of the girder. Stability during lifting can be improved by increasing this value. The height can be increased by the use of gussets or other lifting devices.

Lifting Loop Placement Tolerance

Enter the lateral tolerance for placement of lifting loops at the centerline of the top flange of the girder.

Max Girder Sweep Tolerance

Enter the maximum girder sweep tolerance expected for manufacturers in your area. The sweep tolerance is the horizontal deviation of the girder from end to end along the total length of the girder. The Portland Cement Institute (PCI) recommends a sweep tolerance of 1/8inch per 10 feet = 0.00104

Min Angle of Inclination of Lifting Cable

Enter the minimum expected angle of inclination for the lifting cables as measured from the horizontal. Angled lifting cables induce compression and moment into the girder. This effect is accounted for in the lifting analysis. The worst case is when the lifting angle is 90 degrees. 

Parameters - Design

Min Lifting Point Location from ends of girder

Enter the minimum lifting point location from the ends of the girder. The designer and the fabrication optimization analysis will use this as the starting point for the lifting loops.

Lifting Point Accuracy

Enter the accuracy of the lift points. The designer and the fabrication optimization analysis will use this accuracy when locating the lifting points.
