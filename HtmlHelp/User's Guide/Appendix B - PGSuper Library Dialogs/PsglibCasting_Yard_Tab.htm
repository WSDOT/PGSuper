Casting Yard Tab

Use this tab to define specification-related information that is pertinent to the casting yard stage.

Manufacturing Concerns

This data is dependent on local materials and the capabilities of local precasters

Strand Slope

Enter the maximum allowable strand slopes for 12.7mm (0.5”) diameter and smaller strands, or 15.24mm (0.6”) strands. Slope values are entered as the horizontal component. Hence a slope change of 1:6 would be entered as 6.

Checking and designing for maximum strand slopes can be enabled or disabled on the Design Tab.

Hold Down Force

This value is used to insure that the maximum allowable hold-down force at the harping point is not exceeded. This value will be dependent on the facilities of the precast manufacturers in your area.

Checking and designing for maximum hold down force can be enabled or disabled on the Design Tab.

Curing Method

Select whether a normal (moist) or accelerated (steam or radiant heat) curing method is used. This will affect creep calculations per LRFD 5.4.2.3.2.

Curing Equivalence

Enter the equivalent number of days of normal curing for accelerated curing. This is used with the initial time of loading in creep and creep loss computations.

NOTE: For AASHTO LRFD Third Edition 2004 with 2005 interim provisions and later editions, the equivalency was removed from the specifications. This is believed to be an editorial omission. From NCHRP Report 496, page 9, "ti = age of concrete, in days, when load is initially applied for accelerated curing or the age minus 6 days for moist curing".

When the Project Criteria is based on AASHTO LRFD Third Edition 2004 or earlier editions, the curing equivalence is added to the age at initial loading when accelerated curing is used.

When the Project Criteria is based on AASHTO LRFD Third Edition 2004 with 2005 interim provisions and later editions, the curing equivalence is subtracted from the age at initial load when normal curing is used.

Allowable Concrete Stresses

Compressive Stress

Enter the multiplier for the maximum allowable concrete compressive stress for Service I limit state loads in the casting yard. Refer to Section 5.9.4.1.1 in the AASHTO LRFD Specifications for the recommended value.

Tensile Stress

Enter the multiplier for the maximum allowable concrete tensile stress for Service I limit state loads in the casting yard. Refer to Section 5.9.4.1.2 in the AASHTO LRFD Specifications for the recommended value.

Anchorage Zone

Splitting Zone Length

Enter a value "N" that will be used to compute the length of the splitting (bursting) zone for LRFD 5.10.10.1. The length of the zone is defined as h/N where h is the overall height of the bare girder. N is typically 4 or 5. 

Note that the anchorage zone check can be disabled from the Design Tab.
