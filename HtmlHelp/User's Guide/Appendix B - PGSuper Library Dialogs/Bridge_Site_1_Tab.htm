Bridge Site 1 Tab

Use this tab to define criteria pertinent at bridge site stage 1. Stage 1 occurs when the non-composite girder is erected onto its permanent piers. Stresses are checked when temporary strands are removed prior to slab and diaphragm casting. Stresses are also checked after the slab and diaphragms have been cast. Slab material has weight, but no strength (the girder is noncomposite).

Girder stress evaluation at this stage is not required by AASHTO. However some bridge owners do require stress limitations.

Evaluate stress limits for temporary loading conditions

Check this box to enable the evaluation of these stress limits.

Allowable Concrete Stresses - Temporary Strand Removal Stage

Allowable concrete stress limits used for evaluating girder stresses just after temporary strand removal

Compressive Stress

Enter the multiplier to determine the maximum allowable concrete compressive stress for Service I limit state loads at Bridge Site Stage 1. Refer to Section 5.9.4.1.1 in the AASHTO LRFD Specifications for the recommended value.

Tensile Stress

Enter the multiplier to determine the maximum allowable concrete tensile stress for Service I limit state loads at Bridge Site Stage 1. A maximum value for the allowable stress can be specified. Refer to Section 5.9.4.1.2 in the AASHTO LRFD Specifications for the recommended value.

Constructability

Check Bottom Flange Clearance

Check the box to enable bottom flange clearance checks. When enabled, the clear distance between bottom flanges will be computed and compared to the minimum bottom flange clearance specified in this setting. Owners may want to use this option to alert engineers to situations where the clearance between bottom flanges may cause construction or inspection access issues.
