Bridge Site 2 Tab

Use this tab to define criteria pertinent at bridge site stage 2. Stage 2 is when the slab has cured and superimposed dead loads have just been added to the bridge.

Allowable Concrete Stresses

Compressive Stress

Enter the multiplier to determine the maximum allowable concrete compressive stress for Service I limit state loads at Bridge Site Stage 2. Refer to Section 5.9.4.1.1 in the AASHTO LRFD Specifications for the recommended value.

Distribution of Superimposed Dead Load

Distribution of Railing System Load

Enter the maximum number of girders, webs, or mating surfaces to distribute the railing system loads. The railing system loads include the dead load of the exterior traffic barrier, sidewalk, and combination railing as well as pedestrian live load on sidewalk.

For a more detailed discussion on the distribution of railing system loads, see the Structural Analysis Models topic in the Theoretical Manual.

A mating surface is the contact point between a girder and the bridge deck. Examples are:

Distribution of Overlay Load

LRFD 4.6.2.2.1 states "permanent loads of and on the deck may be distributed uniformly among the beams and/or stringers". However, many agencies prefer to distribute overlay loads using tributary area. Select your preference here.

Composite Section

Select to compute the tributary width of the slab using LRFD 4.6.2.6, or to override LRFD and always use the tributary width of the slab.
