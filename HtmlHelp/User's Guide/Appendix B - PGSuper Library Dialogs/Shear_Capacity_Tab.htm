Shear Capacity Tab

Shear Capacity

Select the method by which to compute shear capacity. The available methods depend upon the design criteria basis specified on the Description tab. The following options are available:

Design Criteria Basis

Shear Capacity Method

1st Edition, 2004 through 2nd Edition, 1998 with 1999 interim provisions

Compute in accordance with LRFD 5.8.3.4.2 (General Method)

2nd Edition, 1998 with 2000 interim provisions through 3rd Edition, 2004 with 2005 - 2006 interim provisions

Compute in accordance with LRFD 5.8.3.4.2 (General Method)
Compute in accordance with WSDOT Bridge Design Manual (June 2001)

4th Edition, 2007

Compute in accordance with LRFD 5.8.3.4.2 (General Method)
Compute in accordance with LRRD 5.8.3.4.3 (Vci Vcw Method)
Compute in accordance with WSDOT Bridge Design Manual (June 2001)
Compute in accordance with WSDOT Bridge Design Manual (August 2007)

4th Edition, 2007 with 2008 interim provision and later

Compute in accordance with LRFD 5.8.3.4.2 (General Method)
Compute in accordance with LRRD 5.8.3.4.3 (Vci Vcw Method)
Compute in accordance with LRFD B5.1 (Beta-Theta Tables)
Compute in accordance with WSDOT Bridge Design Manual (June 2001)
Compute in accordance with WSDOT Bridge Design Manual (August 2007)

NOTE: The LRFD 5.8.3.4.2 (General Method) dramatically changed with the 2008 interim provisions. The General Method prior to 2008 became the LRFD B5.1 (Beta-Theta Tables) method in the 2008 interim provisions. The 2008 interim provisions change the General Method to what is commonly known as the Canadian Standards Association (CSA) method.

NOTE: For 4th Edition, 2007 with 2008 interim provision and later the WSDOT BDM August 2007 method is the same as LRFD 5.8.3.4.2 (General Method).

Modulus of Rupture

The Modulus of Rupture is used in the Vci Vcw Method (LRFD 5.8.3.4.3). Enter a multiplier for sqrt(f'ci) for Normal weight, Sand lightweight, and All lightweight concrete.

Resistance Factors

Enter resistance factors for normal weight and lightweight concrete.

Longitudinal Reinforcement for Shear

Select whether to check LRFD Provision 5.8.3.5 at all sections of the girder

The longitudinal reinforcement can be accounted for.

Horizontal Interface Shear

Select the method for computing horizontal interface shear. Interface shear can be computed using the classical shear flow formula (VQ)/(Ib) or using the LRFD simplified method V/(bd)
