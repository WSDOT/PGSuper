Creep Tab

General Creep Parameters

Time from stressing to prestress transfer

Enter the number of hours from the time of stressing of the prestress strands to prestress transfer. This parameter is used to compute the initial relaxation loss prior to prestress transfer.

Time from prestress transfer until temporary strand removal/diaphragm casting

Enter the expected timing range from prestress transfer until temporary strands are removed, or in the case of no temporary strands, when intermediate diaphragms are constructed.

Time from prestress transfer until slab casting or application of superimposed dead loads on noncomposite girders

Enter the expected timing range from prestress transfer until the slab is cast or superimposed dead loads are applied in the case of noncomposite girders.

Total Creep Duration

Enter the total duration of creep from time of prestress transfer.

The construction timing has a large influence over the creep behavior of a prestressed girder bridge. Given that real life is full of variation that cannot be controlled at design time, it is difficult to accurately predict the exact construction timing. If girders are erected sooner than anticipated the camber will likely be much less and additional haunch concrete will be needed to raise the deck to match profile grade. Conversely, if girders are erected later than expected, excessive camber may result.

WSDOT as adopted a methodology of computing creep and camber based on a minimum timing and the maximum permissible timing of construction. By publishing the range of camber in the contract drawings, the contractor has some idea as to the variability of camber and the haunch concrete requirements and can use this information in making choices about the construction schedule.

Curing Equivalence

Enter the equivalent number of days of normal curing for steam or radiant heat curing. This is used with the initial time of loading in creep and creep loss computations.
