Strands

Use the Strands tab on the Girder Details dialog to describe the filling of strands, jacking forces, type of strands, and vertical adjustment of adjustable prestressing strand locations.

Describing Prestressing Strands in a Girder

Pretensioned prestressing is described by filling allowable locations of straight, adjustable, and temporary prestressing strands, and the jacking forces imparted onto the strands. Locations and harping of adjustable strands can be further defined by vertical adjustment of the strand pattern.

Defining strands in PGSuper is a two step process: First; available locations, possible types, and fill order for strands must be defined by the strand grids in a girder's Girder Library entry. Once defined, locations can be filled and adjusted using the Girder Details dialog above.

Basic Types of Strands

 PGSuper provides input for three basic types of strands:

  1. Straight Permanent Strands can be placed anywhere within a girder section and are straight along the entire girder. "Permanent" means that these strands are jacked in the casting yard with the prestressing remaining for the life of the girder.

  2. Adjustable Strands are permanent strands that have the unique capability to be adjusted vertically within a girder's web region. Adjustable strands can only be placed within the thinnest portion of a girder section's web(s), and can optionally be adjusted vertically continuously within the bounds defined by the girder library entry. There are two possible types of Adjustable Strands: Harped or Straight-web strands. Harped strands are draped at the girder's harping points, and Straight web strands are straight along the entire length of the girder.

    Harping (draping) is created by vertical adjustment of the strand group at the girder ends or harping points.

  3. Temporary Strands are straight strands that can be placed anywhere within a girder. Temporary strands can be pretensioned or post-tensioned. Pretensioned strands are tensioned prior to casting. Post-tensioned temporary strands can be applied to the girder either immediately before lifting, immediately after lifting, or immediately before shipping. All Temporary Strands are removed after shipping (before Bridge Site Stage 1). Temporary strands post-tensioned immediately before shipping are assumed to have no impact on camber and losses.

Selection of Adjustable Strand Type

As mentioned above, adjustable strands can be either harped or straight. The possible selections for a given girder are defined in its girder library entry.

Filling of Prestressing Strands

There are two basic methods for filling prestressing strand locations: 1) Enter a number strands - Strands are filled sequentially using the order defined by the pertinent girder library entry. 2) Direct selection of available strand locations using the "Select Strands" dialog. This allows strands to be filled out of order.

Entering Number of Strands (Sequential Strand Fill)

For this method, Strands are filled sequentially using the permanent strand fill order defined in the girder's library entry. Strand 1 goes in position 1, strand 2 goes in position 2, and so on.

To enter the number of strands, simply use the Up/Down arrow buttons on the dialog to dial-in the desired number of strands. The number of strands is limited to the potential strand locations defined by the Girder Library Entry. The number of strands may be specified as the Number of Straight and Number of adjustable (Harped or Straight-web) or as the Total Number of Strands.

Direct Selection of Strand Locations

This method allows filling of strands by selecting individual strand locations in the "Select Strands" dialog.

Note: The automated design algorithm always fills strands using sequential strand fill.

Entering Jacking Forces

You can explicitly enter a jacking force for each group (straight, adjustable, or temporary) of prestressing strands, or you can let PGSuper compute the maximum allowable jacking force based on LRFD 5.9.3. Explicitly entering the jacking force is useful when evaluating a design or existing structure.

To explicitly enter the jacking force:

  1. Remove the check from "Calculate Jacking Force"

  2. Enter the jacking force

To let PGSuper compute the maximum allowable jacking force

  1. Check the Calculate Jacking Force option.

Note : PGSuper will use the average jacking stress for all strands in the section when performing calculations.

Vertical Location of Adjustable Strands (Harped or Straight-web)

If allowed by the library entry; you can adjust the vertical location of adjustable harped strands at the end of the girder and/or at the harping point, or for adjustable straight-web strands along the girder. These adjustments are typically made to control stresses. For example: In the casting yard stage, if there is excessive compressive stress at the top of the girder, near the ends, adjustable strands could be lowered to relieve the stress. Likewise, if there is excessive compressive stress at the bottom of the girder, between harping points, the harping points can be raised to relieve the stress. The basis location of adjustable strands can be described by:

  • Distance between the CG of the Web Strand Force and the Top of the Girder

  • Distance between the CG of the Web Strand Force and the Bottom of the Girder

  • Distance between the Top-Most Web Strand and the Top of the Girder

  • Distance between the Top-Most Web Strand and the Bottom of the Girder

  • Distance between the Bottom-Most Web Strand and the Bottom of the Girder

  • Eccentricity of the Web Strand Group as measured on the non-composite section

Note: Adjustable strands must lie within the vertical bounds defined by the adjustment bounds defined in the girder library entry.

Note: Hold the CTRL key down while changing the basis of measurement and the input value will be converted to the new basis.
