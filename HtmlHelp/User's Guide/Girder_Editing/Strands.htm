Strands

On the Strands tab, describe the number and location of the prestressing strands.

Strand Details

Prestressing is described by the number of harped (or straight-web), straight, and temporary prestressing strands, and the jacking force imparted onto the strands. Locations of harped (or straight-web) strands can be further defined by vertical adjustment of the strand pattern at the end of the girder or at the harping point.

Allowable locations for strands are defined in the Girder Library for the pertinent girder library entry. PGSuper allows input of three basic types of strands.

  1. Straight Permanent Strands can be placed anywhere within a girder section and are straight along the entire girder. "Permanent" means that these strands are jacked in the casting yard with the prestressing remaining for the life of the girder.

  2. Web Strands are permanent strands that can only be placed within the thinnest portion of a girder section's web(s). Web Strands have the unique capability to be vertically adjusted within the web region. There are two types of Web Strands: Harped Strands and Straight-Web strands. Harped strands are draped at the girders harping points, and Straight-Web strands are straight along the entire length of the girder.

  3. Temporary Strands are straight strands that can be placed anywhere within a girder. The strands can be pretensioned in the casting yard or posttensioned at various stages in the girder's life and are removed after shipping.

Entering Number of Strands

To enter the number of strands, simply use the Up/Down arrow buttons to dial-in the desired number of strands. The number of strands are limited to the number of potential strand locations defined for the Girder Library Entry in use. The number of strands may be specified as the Number of Straight and Number of Harped or Straight-Web (WSDOT method), or as the Total Number of Strands (TxDOT method).

Strands are filled in the order prescribed by the permanent strand fill order in the girder's library entry.

Entering Jacking Force

You can explicitly enter a jacking force for each group (straight, web, temporary) of the prestressing strands, or you can let PGSuper compute the maximum allowable jacking force based on LRFD 5.9.3. Explicitly entering the jacking force is useful when evaluating a design or existing structure.

To explicitly enter the jacking force:

  1. Remove the check from "Calculate Jacking Force"

  2. Enter the jacking force

To let PGSuper compute the maximum allowable jacking force

  1. Check the Calculate Jacking Force option.

Note : PGSuper will use the average jacking stress for all strands in the section when performing calculations.

Temporary Strands

Temporary strands can be pretensioned or post-tensioned. Pretensioned strands are built into the girder and are effective during lifting and shipping. Post-tensioned temporary strands can be applied to the girder either immediately before lifting, immediately after lifting, or immediately before shipping. Temporary strands are assumed to be removed after shipping. Temporary strands post-tensioned immediately before shipping are assumed to have no impact on camber and losses.

Use the drop down list to describe how temporary strands are used.

Vertical Location of Web (Harped or Straight-Web) Strands

If allowed by the library entry; you can adjust the vertical location of harped strands at the end of the girder or at the harping point, or straight-web strands along the girder to control stresses. For example: In the casting yard stage, if there is excessive compressive stress at the top of the girder, near the ends, the web strands could be lowered to relieve the stress. Likewise, if there is excessive compressive stress at the bottom of the girder, between harping points, the harping points can be raised to relieve the stress. The basis location of the web strands can be described by:

  • Distance between the CG of the Web Strand Force and the Top of the Girder

  • Distance between the CG of the Web Strand Force and the Bottom of the Girder

  • Distance between the Top-Most Web Strand and the Top of the Girder

  • Distance between the Top-Most Web Strand and the Bottom of the Girder

  • Distance between the Bottom-Most Web Strand and the Bottom of the Girder

  • Eccentricity of the Web Strand Group as measured on the non-composite section

Note: Web strands must lie within the vertical bounds defined by the adjustment bounds defined in the girder library entry.

Note: Hold the CTRL key down while changing the basis of measurement and the input value will be converted to the new basis.
