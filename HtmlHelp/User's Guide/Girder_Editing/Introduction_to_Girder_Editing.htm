Chapter 4 - Girder Editing

In this chapter you will learn about the Girder View and how to edit the girders in your bridge.

Each girder in the bridge can have a different section type and each girder in the bridge can have different configurations (such as span length, concrete properties, strand configuration, and handling points). Geometric effects like piers with opposing skews or horizontal curvature cause the girders within a span to be different length. Girders of differing lengths potentially require different prestressing, shear reinforcement, concrete properties, etc.

Each girder's properties are edited individually using the Girder Details Dialog. Each tab of the dialog focuses on editing a different attribute of the girder, such as section and materials, strands, reinforcement, and location of lifting devices. For those cases when all of the girders in your structure are the same, PGSuper provides a tool to copy the properties of one girder to another, simplifying the editing task.

The Girder View provides a visual representation of the girders in your bridge, as well as context-sensitive editing capabilities.
