Standard Loading

PGSuper automatically generates all of the standard loading for a precast-prestressed girder bridge. These loading conditions include:

  • Self-weight dead load of the girders, deck, diaphragms, and the bridge railings

  • HL93 Live Load

  • Fatigue Live Load (AASHTO LRFD 2009 and later)

  • Pedestrian live load on Sidewalk

  • Overlays

Dead loads are based on cross sectional area of the members and the material unit weight. See the Structural Analysis Models section of the Theoretical Manual for a detailed explanation of how PGSuper builds analysis models and applies loads.

A construction load allowance that will be carried by the non-composite girder section can also be defined. This allowance covers additional loads incurred by the use of stay-in-place metal forms or other permanent construction materials and tolerances. This loading is applied to the entire bridge deck and is distributed to each girder based on its tributary area. This loading is applied to the DC load case.

Distribution of Exterior and Interior Barrier Loads

Barrier dead loads are distributed to the "N nearest Girders, Mating Surfaces, or Webs (GMSW’s) as defined on the Bridge Site 2 tab in the Project Critieria library. An exact definition is as follows:

Distribute the weight of the barrier evenly to the N nearest Girders, Mating Surfaces, or Webs (GMSW’s). Nearest distance is measured from the C.G. of the barrier in a bridge cross section taken at mid-span. For cases when the weight of a barrier can be distributed to either of two GMSW’s that are equal distance left and right of the barrier C.G., and these GMSW’s are furthest from the barrier, the load will be distributed to the exterior-most GMSW. If the span contains 2N or fewer GMSW’s, the railing load will be distributed evenly to all GMSW’s.

Distribution of Sidewalk Dead Load

Sidewalk loads are distributed to the "N nearest Girders, Mating Surfaces, or Webs (GMSW’s) as defined on the Bridge Site 2 tab in the Project Critieria library. An exact definition is as follows:

Distribute the sidewalk weight and pedestrian live load evenly to the greater of: all Girders, Mating Surfaces, or Webs (GMSW’s) lying directly under the sidewalk; or the N nearest GMSW’s measured from centerline sidewalk using a bridge cross section taken at mid-span. For cases when the sidewalk weight can be distributed to either of two GMSW’s that are equal distance left and right of C.L. sidewalk, and these GMSW’s are furthest from the C.L. sidewalk, the load will be distributed to the exterior-most GMSW. If the span contains 2N or fewer GMSW’s, the load will be distributed evenly to all GMSW’s.

Distribution of Pedestrian Live Load

The input sidewalk width is always used to compute pedestrian live load magnitude. Pedestrian live load is distributed to nearby girders in the same manner as sidewalk dead load. Pedestrian live load is computed in accordance with LRFD 3.6.1.6.

The magnitude of the uniform pedestrian live load is specified on the Bridge Site 2 tab in the Project Criteria library.

Distribution of  Overlay Loads

The exterior edge of the overlay terminates at the interior-most of:

  1. Interior toe of Exterior Barrier

  2. Face of sidewalk (if no interior barrier)

  3. Interior toe of Interior Barrier

The magnitude of the overlay load is computed using the overlay width, thickness, and density of the overlay material. Options to distribute the overlay load are specified on the Bridge Site 2 tab in the Project Criteria library.
