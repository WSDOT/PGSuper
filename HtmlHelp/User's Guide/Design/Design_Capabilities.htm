Design Capabilities

There are many parameters involved when designing a precast girder bridge, including girder spacing, girder size, span length, slab depth, slab overhang width, and concrete strength. In the most general case, you could say, "There's a hole in the ground, design a bridge that spans it". As you can see, design is a very open-ended activity.

The automated design feature in PGSuper has a more narrow scope. Given basic bridge configuration information, it will make an estimate at what some of the input parameters should be for a selected girder. The automated design tool can design for flexure and shear.

For flexure, PGSuper will determine input for:

  • Number of Straight and Harped Strands

  • Jacking Force of Straight and Harped Strands

  • Harped Strand Offset at End of Girder and at Harping Point

  • Concrete Release Strength, f'ci

  • Concrete 28 Day Strength, f'c

  • Slab Offset, Haunch ("A" Dimension)

  • Location of Lifting Loops for girder handling

  • Location of Truck Support for girder transportation

  • Number and jacking force for Temporary Prestressing Strands to control excess camber and girder stability.

For shear, PGSuper will guess input for

  • Vertical Shear Reinforcement

  • Horizontal Interface Shear

  • Splitting/Bursting Reiforcement

  • Confinement Reinforcement

  • Longitudinal Reinforcement for Shear

NOTES:

  1. Design considerations for Slab Offset, Lifting, Hauling, Strand Hold Down, Strand Slope, Splitting, and Confinement are optional. These options may be turned off and on via the Design Tab in the Project Criteria library.

  2. Parameters that control transverse reinforcement design are located on the  Shear Design Tab in the girder library.
