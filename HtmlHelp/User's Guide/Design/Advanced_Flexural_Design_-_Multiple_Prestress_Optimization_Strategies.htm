Advanced Flexural Design - Multiple Prestress Optimization Strategies

For most prestress girder designs, strands and/or material properties must be adjusted in order to meet stress requirements near the ends of a girder. In PGSuper, this is called Prestressing Optimization. PGSuper's prestressing optimization strategies fall into these basic categories:

  1. Increasing concrete strength

  2. Debonding of strands

  3. Harping of strands

  4. Addition of highly-placed (raised), straight fully-bonded strands along the entire girder length

The choice of one or more optimization strategies depends on the girder type, economic preferences, safety, or other concerns in your local area. For example, local precasters might prefer debonding over harping: So for I-girders an agency might first want to attempt an all-straight fully bonded design using a high strength concrete; if that fails, try a debond design with medium strength concrete; and if that fails, try a harped strand design.

Note: The use of temporary strands may also be considered a prestressing optimization strategy. However, the automated design algorithm will only add temporary strands for the lifting and hauling conditions. This strategy is automatically enabled if a girder contains temporary strands.

Cascading Prestress Optimization Strategies

Prestress Optimization strategies are configured on the Flexural Design tab for a given Girder Library entry as shown below. Here, you can configure PGSuper's Girder Designer to attempt different types of prestressing designs in the order shown on the dialog.

  • Strategies are attempted in the order shown on the dialog. When a strategy fails, the algorithm will proceed to the next strategy until all have been exhausted

  • Concrete strength limits must be specified for each strategy, and the strategy will fail if the required concrete strength exceeds the limits

  • Release strength must be at least 4.0 KSI, and Final Strength must be at least 5.0 KSI

Configuring Permanent Strands for Prestress Optimization

A prestress optimization strategy can only be performed if prestressing strands are available for the strategy type. For instance, a debonded strand design can only be performed if the girder's library entry contains debondable straight strand locations. Strand types and their locations are specified on the girder library's Permanent Strands Tab.

Here are some basic rules for setting up strands for design strategies:

  • The algorithm can switch between straight, debond, raised, and harped designs if the adjustable strand type is set to "Straight or Harped" in the library.

  • A harped strand design cannot be attempted if no harped strands exist

  • Debond strand design cannot be attempted if there are no straight strands to debond

  • Raised strand strategies can only be attempted if there are adjustable straight strands located above the girder's neutral axis

  • The algorithm will not attempt to adjust raised straight strands by translating them vertically (i.e., strands are placed only in their location specified in the strand grid).
