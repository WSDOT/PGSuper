Chapter 8 - Design

In this chapter, we will discuss PGSuper's design capabilities and will discuss the procedure for performing a design.

PGSuper is primarily an analysis and specification checking tool. As you will see in the next chapter, PGSuper produces graphs and reports based upon the current bridge and girder descriptions. This is great if you know everything about the structure at the beginning of your project. When designing a new structure, life isn't quite so simple. It is your job as the designer to determine many of the bridge and girder description parameters. This is where PGSuper's automated design feature comes in.

PGSuper is geared toward the detailed and final design stages of a project. Given the general bridge configuration, girder type, framing plan, and material information, PGSuper can make a really good estimate at what the other input parameters should be, including number and configuration of prestressing strands, jacking force, concrete release strength, and transverse reinforcement.

The LRFD design criteria is very complex and there are many criterion that a girder must pass before a design is satisfactory. The design algorithm uses the current basic bridge geometry, materials, and project criteria to design the selected girder.
