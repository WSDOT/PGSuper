Defining the Deck Geometry and Materials

Describing the Bridge Deck

The bridge deck is described by several parameters including its gross depth, plan view geometry, and slab offset (distance from the top of girder to top of slab measured at the intersection of the centerline of bearing and the centerline of girder - this is know as the "A" dimension in WSDOT jargon). Roadway surfacing materials are also described for the slab, including overlays and sacrificial wearing depths. The slab material is also specified.

 

Gross Depth

The overall depth of the slab, including the sacrificial depth

Overhang Edge Depth

Depth of slab at edge of overhang. Use the drop down list to select the overhang taper.

Overhang taper

Select the geometry of the overhang taper

Fillet

Precast girders typically have a "built-in" curvature in the vertical plane due to camber. At the mid-point of the girder, camber reduces the slab haunch formed by the slab offset. When computing dead loads, PGSuper assumes the depth of the slab haunch to be constant across the entire length of the girder.

Use same Slab Offset for entire bridge

When checked, a single slab offset is used for the entire bridge. Otherwise the slab offset may be defined at both ends of every span or girder.

Slab Offset ("A" Dimension)

The slab offset is the distance from the top of the girder to the top of the slab at the intersection of the centerline of the girder and the centerline of bearing (at both ends of the span). PGSuper assumes the slab offset is the same for all girders at all piers. In WSDOT jargon, the slab offset is known as the "A" dimension.

Wearing Surface

Select the type of wearing surface. Future overlay are considered as final dead load and does not effect loss calculations.

Overlay Weight

When selected, the overlay is defined by a uniform pressure loading

Overlay Depth

When selected, the overlay is defined by a depth and unit weight/density of the overlay material. PGSuper assumes the overlay depth is constant over the entire bridge deck. The overlay is assumed to be between curb lines.

Overlay Density

Density of the overlay material. Used for computing self-weight.

Sacrificial Depth

A depth of slab that is assumed to be worn away over time due to friction and abrasion. The portion of the slab within the sacrificial depth is not used in the calculation of section properties or structural capacity.

Plan (Edge of Deck)

The edge of deck is defined by a series of points and transitions. The deck edge points are left and right offsets at a given station. The offsets can be measured from the Bridge Line or from the Alignment.

The deck edge is assumed to be parallel to the alignment before and after the first and last edge points. The offsets are the same as used for the first and last points.

Between points the deck edge can transition linearly or with a cubic spline. The deck edge can also be parallel to the alignment.
