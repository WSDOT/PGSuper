Railing System

The bridge railing system is described on the Railing System tab. The railing system consists of an exterior barrier (located at the edge of the deck), and optionally a sidewalk and interior barrier (located at the edge of the sidewalk).

The barriers are described in the Traffic Barrier Library.

Select an exterior barrier from the list.

Optionally add a sidewalk. The back depth is the depth of the sidewalk closest to the exterior barrier. The face depth is the depth of sidewalk at the face of curb when an interior barrier is not used or the depth at the interior barrier when used.

NOTE: Sidewalk pedestrian live load is automatically enabled. See Chapter 5 and the Bridge with Sidewalks tutorial.

Barriers may be placed on top of sidewalks if the option is selected. Otherwise the sidewalk is placed between barriers.

The sidewalk may be considered as a structurally continuous component for purposes of computing live load deflections per LRFD 2.5.2.6.2

Select an interior barrier from the list.

If a sidewalk is used, or if either the exterior or interior barrier require Concrete Parameters, they are entered in this dialog.
