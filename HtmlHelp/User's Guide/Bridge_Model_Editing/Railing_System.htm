Railing System

The bridge railing system is described on the Railing System tab. The railing system consists of an exterior barrier (located at the edge of the deck), and optionally a sidewalk and interior barrier (located at the edge of the sidewalk).

The barriers are described in the Traffic Barrier Library.

Select an exterior barrier from the list.

Optionally add a sidewalk. The back depth is the depth of the sidewalk closest to the exterior barrier. The face depth is the depth of sidewalk at the face of curb when an interior barrier is not used or the depth at the interior barrier when used.

Barriers may be placed on top of sidewalks if the option is selected. Otherwise the sidewalk is placed between barriers.

The sidewalk may be considered as a structurally continuous component for purposes of computing live load deflections per LRFD 2.5.2.6.2

Select an interior barrier from the list.

If a sidewalk is used, or if either the exterior or interior barrier require Concrete Parameters, they are entered in this dialog.

Definition of Railing System Geometry

This section provides precise detailed definitions for the geometry.

Barrier Geometry

Barriers are defined in the Barrier Library. A barrier shape is a polygon consisting of X,Y points that define its exterior boundary. Barrier geometry is defined as on the left side of the bridge looking ahead station (right barriers are simply mirrored). The figure below shows a polygon that might define a complex barrier shape:

  •  The origin (X=0.0, Y=0.0) serves to locate barriers vertically and horizontally.

    • For all barriers, the Y origin is placed vertically at the top of deck, or optionally at the top of sidewalk if the barrier is placed on top of the sidewalk.

    • For exterior barriers, the X origin is placed horizontally at the left edge of deck, or left edge of exterior girder if no deck is present.

  • The Exterior Edge is the minimum X location on the polygon

  • The Interior Edge is the maximum X location on the polygon

Curb Line

The Curb Line locates the edge of roadway and is located by the Curb Offset distance from the Interior Edge of the barrier polygon. A positive Curb Offset locates the Curb Line to the left of the Interior Edge of the barrier as shown in the previous figure.

Toe of Barrier

The toe of barrier is the width of the barrier measured along a horizontal line at Y=0.0. This value is computed based on the barrier shape geometry. Adjacent sidewalks and overlays extend to the toe of barrier for dead load calculation purposes.

Rules for Layout of Barriers, Sidewalks, and Roadway Dimensions

Layout of Exterior Barriers

The Barrier origin point is used to locate exterior barriers.

  • The barrier X origin is placed horizontally at the left edge of deck, or left edge of top of exterior girder if no deck is present.

  • The barrier Y origin is placed vertically at the top of deck (or girder), or at the top of sidewalk if the barrier is placed on top of the sidewalk.

  • The edge of roadway cannot be outboard of the edge of deck. The Barrier library dialog will issue an error if this occurs.

Back Depth and Face Depth are measured at the toes of the adjacent barriers, or at the edge of sidewalk if no interior barrier exists.

 

 

Layout of Interior Barriers

If an interior barrier exists, its exterior edge is always located at the interior edge of the input sidewalk width as shown in the previous figures. The Y origin of the interior barrier is placed at top of the deck, or at the top of sidewalk if the barrier is placed on top of the sidewalk.

NOTE: Sidewalk pedestrian live load is automatically computed if a sidewalk is present, but must be enabled on the Edit Live Loads dialog. See Chapter 5 and the Bridge with Sidewalks tutorial.
