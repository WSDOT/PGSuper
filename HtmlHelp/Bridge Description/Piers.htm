Bridge Description - Piers

The location of the piers define where the bridge is along the length of a roadway. The distance between piers and their orientation influence structural geometry, such as girder length.

Piers are described in a grid format. The Add Pier and Remove Pier buttons are used to add and remove piers respectively. New piers are identical to and 100 ft down station from the last pier.

The location of the piers can be quickly laid out using the "Layout by Span Lengths" button. This will present the Layout by Span Lengths dialog where the location of one pier is held constant, and pier-to-pier span lengths are entered. Upon exiting this dialog, the pier stations will be adjusted accordingly.

Station

Enter the station of the centerline of pier or back of pavement seat.

Orientation

Enter the orientation of the pier. The orientation can be a bearing (e.g. N 34 23 32.1 W), a skew angle (e.g. 12 02 34.5 R), or the word "Normal". Skew angles are defined as left (L) or right (R).

Connection Geometry

Connections define the location of the centerline of bearing for the girder, the length of end of the girder, end diaphragms, and how the end diaphragm dead load is to be applied to the structural analysis. Connections are defined in the PGSuper library. Select a connection type from the drop down list. 

Connection Boundary Conditions

The connection boundary conditions define the structural support conditions at a pier. Connections can be Hinged, Continuous Before or After Deck Placement, or Integral Before or After Deck Placement.

Continuous boundary conditions assume a hinge between the superstructure and the substructure. Integral boundary conditions assume full fixity between the superstructure and substructure. Select a boundary condition from the drop down list.

The connection geometry and boundary conditions may be the same or different on both sides of a pier. Check the box below the pier description grid to model the connection the same on both sides of a pier.

If this box is unchecked, press the Edit Connection button in the girder to describe connection on the left and right sides of the pier.

NOTE: The boundary conditions may be over-ridden by the Structural Analysis parameter of the Project Criteria. For example, if the Structural Analysis type is Simple Span, then all connections will be treated as hinges regardless of the boundary condition type specified.
