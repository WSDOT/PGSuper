Alignment Description - Horizontal

The geometry of the roadway affects the geometry and loads on the structure. Data in the Alignment Description Dialog describes the horizontal alignment, the vertical profile, and super elevations.

Horizontal Alignment

The horizontal alignment can be either straight or curved.

Straight Alignment

Enter the direction of the alignment as a bearing in the direction of increasing station in the Initial Direction field. Leave the Curve Table empty.

Note: Bearing data must be entered using the standard surveying format of N/S dd mm ss.ss E/W . Trailing zero data need not be entered. For example "N 45 W" is the same as "N 45 00 00.0 W".

Curved Alignment

Enter the initial direction of the alignment in the Initial Direction field. For each horizontal curve in the alignment, add a curve to the Curve Table. Describe each curve with its PI Station, Forward Tangent Bearing or Delta Angle, Radius of the circular curve, and the Entry and Exit lengths of spirals (if present). The initial direction is used as the back tangent of the first curve. The forward tangent of the previous curve is used as the back tangent of the next curve.

A PI angle point can be modeled in the alignment by defining a horizontal curve with a Radius, Entry Spiral Length and Exit Spiral Length of zero.

Horizontal Alignment Control Point

The bridge geometry is modeled in a COGO system within PGSuper. The horizontal alignment control point defined the Northing and Easting of a specific point on the alignment.

Station: Station where the horizontal control point is defined.
East (X): The East/X value of the control point
North (Y): The North/Y value of the control point

NOTE: Coordinates at the start and end of the girders and the points of bearing are given in the Bridge Geometry Report
