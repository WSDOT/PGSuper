Bridge Description - Framing

Define the bridge framing including pier and girder layouts.

Span/Pier Grid

Enter the station and orientation of the piers in the grid. The span lengths are automatically computed and displayed in the grid.

The station can be entered as a decimal value (e.g. 100.00) or in station format (1+00.00).

The pier orientation can be entered as a bearing (e.g. N 34 25 32.23 E), a skew angle (e.g. 34 43 23.2 L), a decimal angle where positive values are right skews (e.g. -30.0), or by the keyword NORMAL (or the abbreviation N) for which the pier will be oriented in the direction of the normal to the alignment.

Press the Edit Details buttons to enter additional details about the piers and spans including connections, boundary conditions, and girder spacing.

Use the Add Span and Remove Span buttons to add and remove spans and piers.

Piers can be laid out by span length. Press the Layout by Span Lengths button to enter span lengths.

Alignment Offset

Enter the distance from the alignment to the bridge reference line. The bridge reference line is a curvilinear path that is at a constant offset from the alignment. The bridge reference line is typically the centerline of bridge, however it could be any convenient line.

The alignment offset is measured normal to the alignment. It may be entered as a decimal where positive values indicate the center of the girder group is to the right of the alignment or entered as a positive value with a Left/Right indicator (e.g. 2.25 L)
