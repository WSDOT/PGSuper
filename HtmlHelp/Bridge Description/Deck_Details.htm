Bridge Description - Deck Details Geometry and Materials

The bridge deck is defined by its cross sectional and plan view geometry as well as its material and wearing surface.

Cross Section

Define the cross sectional geometry of the deck

Gross Depth/Cast Depth

For cast-in-place decks, enter the gross depth of the slab. The gross depth includes the sacrificial depth.

For stay-in-place partial depth deck panels, enter the depth of the cast deck, including sacrificial depth.

Overhang Edge Depth

Enter the gross depth of the deck at the edge of the deck overhang

Overhang Taper

Use the drop down list to describe how the overhang tapers to the exterior girder.

Fillet

Enter the size of the fillet

Slab Offset ("A" Dimension)

Enter the slab offset. The slab offset is taken to be the distance from the top of the girder to the top of the slab at the intersection of the centerline of the girder and the centerline of bearing. "A" Dimension is the WSDOT term for slab offset.

The slab offset is defined for the entire bridge in this dialog. The slab offset can be defined span-by-span on the Span and Pier editing dialogs. The slab offset can be defined girder-by-girder in the Girder editing dialog.

Note: The specification check and design of the "A" dimension can be made either at the time of slab casting, or after the application of superimposed dead loads. This option is available on the Design Tab of the Project Criteria library entry.

Stay-in-Place Deck Panels

This option is available if composite SIP deck panels are used to form the deck.

Panel Depth

Enter the depth of a stay in place deck panel.

Panel Support Width

Enter the width of the girder top flange that supports the deck panel. Twice this width is deduced from the contact surface area used for computing horizontal interface shear capacity.

Plan (Edge of Deck)

Define the plan view geometry of the deck by locating the left and right deck edges. Deck edge points are defined by a Station, Measurement Datum, and Left and Right Offset. Deck edge points can be measured from either the Alignment or the Bridge Line.

Use the Add/Remove buttons to manage the deck points.

When a single deck point is used, the edge of deck will be parallel to the alignment at the specified offset. When multiple deck points are used, a transition between points must be defined. The transition can be linear, spline, or parallel.

A linear transition is simply a straight line connection the deck points.

A spline transition is a cubic curve that passes through deck points.

A parallel transition makes the deck edge parallel to the alignment.

Wearing Surface

Describe the wearing surface.

Wearing Surface Type

Use the drop down list to select the wearing surface type. Choose from Sacrificial Depth of Concrete Deck, Overlay, or Future Overlay.

Overlay Depth

Enter the depth of the overlay.

Overlay Density

Enter the density of the overlay material

Sacrificial Depth

Enter the sacrificial depth.

Note: A sacrificial depth is defined when the wearing surface is Future Overlay to model the amount of the deck that is sacrificed over time until the overlay is added to the structure.

Material

Input the concrete properties or copy them from an existing concrete library entry.
