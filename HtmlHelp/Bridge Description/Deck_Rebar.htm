Bridge Description - Deck Reinforcement

The reinforcement in the bridge deck must be defined to properly perform engineering evaluations of the bridge. The deck reinforcement provides the longitudinal reinforcement in negative moment region of the structure.

PGSuper models longitudinal deck reinforcement as Primary Reinforcement and Supplemental Reinforcement. The primary reinforcement runs the full length of the bridge. The supplemental reinforcement is placed over the piers to supplement the primary reinforcement in resisting negative moments.

Reinforcement

Use the drop down list to select the reinforcing steel used in the deck.

Cover

Enter the cover for the top and bottom mat of reinforcing. The top mat cover is measured from the top of the deck to the center of the reinforcing mat. The bottom mat cover is measured from the bottom (underside) of the deck to the center of the reinforcing mat.

Primary Reinforcement

The primary longitudinal reinforce runs the full length of the bridge. Describe the reinforcement in the top and bottom mats of the deck.

The reinforcement can be described with a bar size and spacing AND a lump sum area per unit length (in the transverse direction).

If both bar spacing and lump sum input is provided, they are added together for the total amount of reinforcement. For example, if the top mat is defined with #6 bars at 18" and 0.18 in2/ft, the total reinforcement will be 0.44in2/1.5ft + 0.18in2/ft = 0.473in2/ft.

Supplemental Reinforcement

The supplemental reinforcement is described similarly to the primary reinforcement. The total supplemental reinforcement is the sum of the lump sum reinforcement and bar size and spacing reinforcement. Supplement reinforcement runs left and right from the centerline of a pier. The left and right cutoff values define how far the reinforcement is extended from the pier.

The total reinforcement at a section is equal to the sum of the primary and supplement reinforcement within the tributary width of a girder.
