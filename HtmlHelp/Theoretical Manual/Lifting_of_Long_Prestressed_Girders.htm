Lifting of Long Prestressed Girders

One of the critical stages in the life of a prestressed girder is when it is first lifted from the casting bed in the casting yard. When the forms are stripped from the girder, the prestressing is higher and the concrete strength is lower than at any other time in the life of the girder. Analyses must be performed for this condition to insure that the girder will not be overstressed, will not crack, and will not fail due to instability.

Calculation of Lifting Stresses

For most girders, critical stresses will occur either at the lifting locations or at the harping points. PGSuper calculates stresses at these points and at tenth-points along the length of the girder.

If the inclination angle q, of the lifting cables is small, this can induce significant compressive force and a concentrated moment into the girder. PGSuper accounts for this effect in the lifting analysis.

Factor of Safety Against Failure - Roll Equlibrium

When a beam hangs from flexible cables, it is free to roll. The roll center is where the cable attaches to the rigid body, which may be a lifting bracket or simply a loop at the top of the beam.

If the beam was perfectly straight and the lifting points were placed exactly over the C.G. of the beam, it would hang perfectly plumb. However, imperfections in the beam (sweep tolerances), and slight misplacement of the lifting loops will always cause C.G. of the beam to be slightly misaligned with the roll center making the beam tip by a small angle qi, where:

    qi = initial roll angle in radians = ei/yr, assuming small angles
    ei = intial eccentricity of the C.G. from the roll axis
    yr = vertical distance from the roll center to the C.G.

qi will cause a component of the beam's weight to be applied toward the weak axis of the beam which will cause it to deflect. This weak axis deflection will cause the C.G. to further shift increasing the weak axis force and so on.
