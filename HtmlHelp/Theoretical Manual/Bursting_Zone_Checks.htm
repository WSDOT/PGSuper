Splitting (Bursting) Zone Checks

The reinforcement in the splitting zone is checked in accordance with LRFD 5.10.10.1. The area of shear reinforcement in the splitting zone is computed as

Av = S(Number of Legs)(Bar Size)(Length)/(Spacing)

Length is the length of the stirrups zone that is contained within the splitting zone.

WSDOT standard practice deviates from the LRFD specifications as outlined in the design memo below;

Memorandum

TO: All Design Section Staff

FROM: J. Kapur

DATE: June 18, 2001

SUBJECT: Design Policy Memo – Pretensioned Anchorage Zones

The vertical reinforcement for the bursting resistance of the anchorage zones in the end of pretensioned girders as specified in the AASHTO-LRFD Bridge Design Specifications article 5.10.10.1 shall be uniformly distributed within the distance h/4 from end of the girder, where “h” is the overall depth of precast member.

Background:

The distribution length of h/5 for the vertical in the end of pretensioned girders as specified in the AASHTO-LRFD article 5.10.10.1 is overly conservative and results in impractical spacing of vertical reinforcing at the end of pretensioned girders, particularly for longer spans prestressed girders where higher prestressing forces are required. The distribution zone of h/4 in accordance with the AASHTO Standard Specifications Article 9.22.1 has been successfully used for many years. The AASHTO Subcommittee on Concrete Bridges in their May 2001 meeting in Seattle approved the above modification to the AASHTO-LRFD Bridge Design Specifications article 5.10.10.1.
