Hauling of Long Prestressed Girders - WSDOT Method

A critical stage in the life of a prestressed girder is when it is shipped from the casting yard to the bridge site. The Washington State DOT utilizes a method for hauling analysis that is primarily based on a two-part paper written by Robert F. Mast and published in the PCI Journal. Further citations, and references to other related articles can be found here.

The WSDOT method performs an analysis for the hauling condition to insure that the girder will not be overstressed, will not crack, and that the truck will not roll over.

Factor of Safety Against Cracking

When a beam is supported from below on an elastic support, it has a tendency to roll about a roll axis. To keep the beam from rolling over, the support must be able to provide adequate resistance to rotation. The resistance can be expressed as an elastic rotational spring constant Kq. To determine equilibrium, we can sum moments about the roll center and equate the resisting force of the springs to the applied force.

W [ zo sinq cosq + ei cosq + y sinq] = Kq (q-a)

Tip: A correct value for Kq is crucial for accurate results. You should use values obtained from measurements on real trucks used by contractors in your area.

Factor of Safety Against Roll Over

Tests done by PCI have shown that typical beams can be tilted by over 30 degrees before they fail in weak axis bending. For this reason, it is believed that most beam hauling failures have been caused by instability of hauling rigs, and not beam failure. Catastrophic roll over of the trailer can be caused by inadequate stiffness of the trailer suspension, or by radical superelevation, or most commonly, by both.
