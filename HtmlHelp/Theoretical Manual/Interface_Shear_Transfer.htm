Interface Shear Transfer

PGSuper calculates interface shear transfer resistance using the methods outlined in Section 5.8.4 in the LRFD Specifications.

Definitions

The term Avf is used inconsistently in the LRFD specifications. In some cases Avf is treated as the area of shear reinforcement crossing the shear plane, and in other cases it is treated as reinforcement area/length along the member. To avoid this confusion in PGSuper, we make the following definition:

    Avf = Total area of shear reinforcement crossing the shear plane.

    avf = Area of shear reinforcement per unit length along the girder.

Hence, LRFD Equation 5.8.4.4-1 would be written as:

avf >= 0.35 bv / fy (SI)

avf >= 0.05 bv / fy (US)

Deck Systems

PGSuper supports Cast-In-Place (CIP) reinforced concrete decks and Stay-In-Place (SIP) Deck Panel systems. The following assumptions are made about the permanent net compressive force normal to the shear plane (Pc) and area of concrete engaged in shear transfer (Acv).

For CIP deck systems, Pc is computed using the tributary width of the deck slab. The entire top flange(s) of the girder are assumed to be roughened and participate in the shear transfer.

For SIP deck systems, it is assumed that the top flange(s) is not roughened beneath the deck panel support. The remainder of the top flange(s) is roughened. The cast portion of the deck is assumed to bear on the unroughened portion of the flange(s) and thus does not contribute to Pc. Only the portion of the cast slab directly over the roughened portion of the top flange(s) contribute to Pc. Furthermore, only the roughened portion of the top flange(s) participate in the shear transfer. 

Shear Stress at Deck/Slab Interface

The shear stress at the deck slab interface can be computed by either the LRFD approximate equation v = V/bd or by the classical shear flow equation v = VQ/Ib.

Cohesion and Friction Factors

Cohesion and friction factors are taken from the first bullet item in LRFD 5.8.4.3. If the top flange is not intentionally roughened, the parameters are taken from the fifth bullet item.
