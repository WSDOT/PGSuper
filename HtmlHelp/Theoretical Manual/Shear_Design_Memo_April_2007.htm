SUBJECT: AASHTO LRFD 4th Edition 2007

The AASHTO LRFD 4th Edition 2007 shall be used for all WSDOT bridges and structures.  The General Notes of all Contract Plans printed on or after May 1, 2007 shall call for “The AASHTO LRFD Bridge Design specifications 4th Edition Dated 2007”.   

The following Sections of the LRFD 4th Edition shall not be used in design of WSDOT bridges:

1. LRFD Article 5.8.3.4.3 “Simplified Procedure for Shear design of Prestressed and Non-prestressed Concrete Sections”.  This article refers to the old AASHTO STD Specifications for shear design of prestressed or non-prestressed concrete sections based on Vci and Vcw .  WSDOT will continue with shear design using the modified compression field theory per LRFD Article “5.8.3.4.1 Simplified Procedure for Nonprestressed Sections”, and article “5.8.3.4.1 General Procedure”.  The requirements of the Design Memorandum issued on March 19, 2002 on LRFD Shear Design are still applicable.

2. LRFD Article 5.14.1.4 “Bridges Composed of Simple Span Precast Girders Made Continuous”.  WSDOT will continue to design these types of bridges as simple span for all transient and permanent loads for both simple and continuous spans.  Continuity reinforcement is provided at intermediate piers for negative transient loads and permanent loads applied after completion of bridge deck construction.

Background

The reintroduction of the STD Specifications shear design procedure of Vci and Vcw to 4th Edition was primarily to accommodate those states transitioning to LRFD specifications to meet the October 2007 deadline.  The NCHRP Report 549 “Simplified Shear Design of Structural Concrete Members” shows the competency of both methods.

The LRFD Article 5.14.1.4 allows for some degree of continuity for loads applied on the bridge after the continuity diaphragms have been cast and cured.  This assumption is based on the age of the girder when continuity is established, and degree of continuity at various limit states.  Both degree of continuity and time of continuity diaphragm casting may result in contractual and design issues. WSDOT will continue its conservative way of designing these types of bridges for the envelope of simple span and continuous spans for applicable permanent and transient loads as stated in BDM Table 5.2.2-1
