Defining Girder Spacing

PGSuper offers several methods of defining girder spacing. These options give you a great deal of flexibility, and with flexibility comes complexity. This section will detail the methods of defining girder spacing and their implications on the bridge geometry.

To property position the girders in the bridge, PGSuper needs to know the spacing between girders and the transverse location of the girders.

Girder Spacing

Girder spacing can be measured at the CL Pier or at the CL Bearing. At these locations the girder spacing is measured either normal to the alignment or along the CL Pier/CL Bearing. The methods for defining the girder spacing are:

  1. Measured at and along the centerline pier

  2. Measured normal to the alignment at the centerline pier

  3. Measured at and along the centerline bearing

  4. Measured normal to the alignment at the centerline bearing

Transverse Location of Girders

The girders must be located transversely as well. The transverse location is described by an offset from either the Bridge Line or the Alignment. This offset is measured in the same direction as the girder spacing. The offset can be measured to a specific girder or to the center of the girder spacing.
