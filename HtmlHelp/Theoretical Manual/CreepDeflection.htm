Deflections Due To Creep

Deflections due to creep are computed in accordance with Article 5.4.2.3 of the LRFD Specification. The deflection due to creep is taken as

If the concrete is cured by steam or radiant heat, day of accelerated curing will be taken equal to seven days of normal curing.

In PGSuper, creep is computed in two stages in order to facilitate the camber equations. The first stage of creep is computed during the time from release of the prestressing strands until the time when the temporary strands are cut and the diaphragms are cast. The second creep stage is computed from the end of the first stage until the section is made composite. 

Deviations from AASHTO LRFD 4th Edition, 2007

The AASHTO LRFD 4th Edition, published in 2007, made changes to the computation of the creep coefficient in Article 5.4.2.3.2 that are believed to be editorial errors. These changes are ignored in PGSuper. In effect, PGSuper uses the equations from Article 5.4.2.3.2 of the AASHTO LRFD 3rd Edition with 2006 interim provisions.

The changes to the 4th Edition that are ignored by PGSuper are as follows:

  • The notation for the volume to surface ratio effect was changed from kvs to ks. The notation ks conflicts with ks used in equation C5.4.2.3.2-2. Furthermore, the kvs notation is inconsistently used at the end of the last paragraph in Article 5.4.2.3.2. PGSuper will use the kvs notation.

  • The lower limit of the volume to surface ratio effect was changed from 0.0 to 1.0 in equation 5.4.2.3.2-2 [kvs = 1.45-0.13(V/S) >= 1.0]. The lower limit should be 0.0 as it was in AASHTO LRFD 3rd Edition, 2006. In fact, this value was changed from 1.0 in AASHTO LRFD 3rd Edition, 2005 to 1.0 in 2006 to correct the error. It is believed that the proposed changes to the 2007 AASHTO were based on the language published in the 2005 AASHTO, thus the change to the lower limit was mistakenly interpreted as a new change. PGSuper will use a lower limit of 0.0.
