TxDOT Research Report 0-6374-2

 ”In 2008, TxDOT initiated Project 0-6374 to investigate prestress losses in pretensioned concrete girders. The final report from the project included new prestress loss provisions that were found to be simpler, more conservative, and more precise than the current methods outlined within the AASHTO LRFD Bridge Design Specifications. A reference to the report is below:

“Effect of New Prestress Loss Estimates on Pretensioned Concrete Bridge Girder Design”; David Garber, José Gallardo, Dean Deschenes, David Dunkman, and Oguzhan Bayrak; Report No. FHWA/TX-12/0-6374-2; October 2012, Rev. June 2013, Published August 2013

Elastic Shortening and Computation of fcgp

For elastic shortening at release, the TxDOT 0-6374-2 method assumes a strand stress at release of 0.70 fpu. This simplifies evaluation since iteration is not required and has been shown to generate more accurate results.

This assumption is sensible for low-relaxation strands at normal (e.g., 0.75 fpu) jacking stresses. However, PGSuper allows stress-relieved strands and user-input jacking forces; which means that virtually any jacking stress is possible. Also, the research database appears only to consider beams with common prestressing configurations: beams with temporary strands, or arrangements with strands above the beam neutral axis were not considered. In these cases, the 0.7 fpu assumption could result in incorrect or even negative losses. In order to accommodate for this possibility, and to allow TxDOT engineers to experiment with different methods for computing fcgp, three different options are provided in PGSuper:

  1. Assume that strand stress at release is 0.70 fpu when computing fcgp. However, if the jacking stress is not equal to 0.75 fpu or, if temporary strands exist: use the iterative approach, as defined in the post-2004 AASHTO specifications, to compute elastic shortening losses. When this occurs, a warning message will be posted to the PGSuper Status Center.

  2. Always assume that strand stress at release is 0.70 fpu when computing fcgp. However, if the jacking stress is less than  0.70 fpu (which would result in negative losses), issue an Error message and halt further computation.

  3. Always compute fcgp using the iterative method, as defined in the post-2004 specifications (LRFD C5.9.5.2.3a).

Time Dependent Loss at Shipping

Note: It is TxDOT policy not to consider shipping during design, however this loss method may be used by other agencies where shipping is a consideration.

Shipping occurs at an unknown time between release and deck placement and there is no way to compute time-dependent losses for the new method, even if the time of shipping is known accurately. Therefore, users must define the time-dependent loss at shipping using a lump sum value, or a percentage of the total final time-dependent prestress loss. Checking will not be performed to insure that this value is realistic.
