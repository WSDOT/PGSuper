Longitudinal Reinforcement in Girders

PGSuper allows you to define longitudinal reinforcement at sections along a girder, or along the entire girder. Longitudinal reinforcement is used in the analysis of several limit state conditions in the program including:

  • Ultimate moment capacity (option must be enabled in Project Criteria library - Moment Capacity tab)

  • Longitudinal reinforcement for shear capacity (option must be enabled in Project Criteria library - Shear Capacity tab)

  • To increase allowable tensile stress for the following temporary conditions: release in the casting yard, girder lifting, and girder hauling

Each of these conditions is described in more detail in the sections below.

Ultimate Moment Capacity

PGSuper computes ultimate moment capacity using a strain compatibility method as described here. If longitudinal rebar exists in a section, and the option to use girder longitudinal rebar for moment capacity is enabled, PGSuper will utilize it when computing the ultimate capacity at sections along the girder for positive moment capacity only. The area of partially developed rebar used in the analysis is reduced per LRFD 5.11.2.1.

Note: The value of shear depth, dv, which is used extensively in shear capacity computations, is computed as part of the ultimate moment capacity analysis. Hence, shear capacity can be affected indirectly by the existence of longitudinal reinforcement. We have noted several cases where longitudinal steel reduces dv, which will result in a reduction of vertical shear capacity and may reduce longitudinal reinforcement for shear capacity. This behavior can be quite unexpected.

Tip: The option to use longitudinal reinforcement must be enabled on the Project Criteria library - Moment Capacity tab

Increased Tensile Allowable for Temporary Conditions

LRFD Section 5.9.4.1.2-1 “Temporary Tensile Stress Limits in Prestressed Concrete before Losses, Fully Prestressed Components”  allows tensile stress limits for temporary conditions to be increased if sufficient bonded reinforcing bars and/or prestressing steel are provided to resist the tensile force in the concrete computed assuming an uncracked section. In PGSuper, higher allowable limits may be specified for the following cases: release in the casting yard, girder lifting, and girder hauling. The stress parameters are defined in the Project Criteria library on the appropriate tabs.

PGSuper employs the same basic approach, but also makes the following assumptions:

  1. The actual area of the tension zone is computed based on the location of the neutral (bending) axis and the geometry of the girder cross section.

  2. All girder longitudinal reinforcement is considered (i.e., there is no option to disable the use of longitudinal reinforcement to increase allowables).

  3. However, rebars will only be utilized if they lie on the tension region of the neutral axis of the section as shown in the above figure. The areas of Partially developed bars are reduced linearly by development. Bars on the compression side are not considered.

  4. The area of prestressing strands will not be considered. It is assumed that the stress capacity of prestressing steel is already fully utilized after jacking and losses at release. Hence, only mild steel rebar is considered when computing As for this requirement.
