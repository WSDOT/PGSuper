The Strut-and-tie model shall be employed where applicable.  The Strut-and-tie model is applicable to members where the plane sections assumption of traditional engineering beam theory is not valid.  The Strut-and-tie model as required by LRFD article 5.8.1.1 & 2, applies to components in which the distance from the point of zero shear to the face of the support is less than 2d, or component in which the load causing more than ½ of the shear at the support is closer than 2d from the face of the support.  Other potential application of Strut-and-tie model includes regions adjacent to abrupt changes in cross-section, opening, draped ends, deep beams, corbels, Integral bent caps, C-bent caps, Outrigger bents, deep Footings, and Pile Caps. The use of Strut-and-Tie model shall be in accordance with the requirement of LRFD Article 5.6.3.  

Background:

The AASHTO Subcommittee on Bridges and Structures in their July 2007 meeting adopted the  and  equations to simplify the general procedure of article 5.8.3.4.2.  The current Tables for  and  will be moved to chapter 5 appendix in the 2008 Interims.  

Application of the simplified shear design procedure using =2.0 and =45o for all non-prestressed members is equivalent to the AASHTO Standard Specifications which have been successfully used for many years.  The complication of  and  for non-prestressed members is not warranted.

Until now, the application of strut-and-tie model was optional upon designers’ choice.  This memorandum now makes the use of strut-and-tie model mandatory where it applies.  This becomes consistent with the LRFD specifications and the current industry practice.
