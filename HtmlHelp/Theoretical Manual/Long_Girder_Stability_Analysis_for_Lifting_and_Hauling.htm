Long Girder Stability Analysis for Lifting and Hauling

As girders become longer, lifting and hauling them becomes more and more challenging. In fact, stresses and instability from lifting and handling often become controlling factors when designing beams over 100 feet in length. This section of the theoretical manual is meant to give a brief overview of the methods used by PGSuper to analyze lifting and hauling of long girders. For a full treatment on these topics, we urge you to read the references.

PGSuper performs in-depth analyses to check criteria for lifting and hauling based on methods cited in the PCI Journal and elsewhere as cited. Note that two methods are provided for the analysis of hauling (shipping) the girder to the bridge site. These are designated as the Washington State DOT and Kansas State DOT methods. Each is documented further in this manual.
