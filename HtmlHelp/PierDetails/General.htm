Pier Details - General

The general properties of piers and abutments are described on this tab. Piers and abutments are described as arbitrary reference lines. The reference line is completely described by its location along the horizontal alignment and its orientation. The reference line can be located at the Back of Pavement Seat, Centerline of Bearing, or any other line of your choosing. It is best to choose the reference line based on the information given on the layout sheet of your bridge plans.

Abutments and piers are described with a reference line, called an Abutment Line or Pier Line. The Abutment/Pier Line is located at any station along the alignment and has a specified orientation. The Abutment/Pier Line is usually selected such that it represents a meaningful line on the abutment or pier such as the back of pavement seat (BPS), end of wearing surface (EWS), centerline of pier, or centerline of bearing. It is best to choose the reference line based on information given in bridge plans.

Station

Enter the location of the reference line as a station along the horizontal alignment.

If the station is changed from a previously defined value, use the drop down list to specify how the bridge is to be adjusted when this pier is moved from one station to another.

Orientation

Enter the orientation of the reference line. The orientation may be entered as the keyword "NORMAL", a bearing of the form N|S dd mm ss.s E|W, or a skew angle of the form dd mm ss.s L|R.

Examples

Normal to alignment

NORMAL

Bearing

N 45 34 43.2 E

Skew Angle

11 25 36.4 R

 

NOTE: If it looks like you've entered a correct bearing or skew angle but PGSuper gives you an error message, verify that the input is in the correct format and that this is only one space between each element.
