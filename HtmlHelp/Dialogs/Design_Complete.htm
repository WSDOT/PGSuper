Preliminary Designer Attempt Complete

After the PGSuper Preliminary Girder Designer has completed its design attempt, you are presented with the Preliminary Design Complete dialog. From here, you can view the outcome of the design attempt and choose whether you will accept the design PGSuper has come up with.

Design Outcome Report

The report in this dialog tells you the outcome of the design by the Preliminary Girder Designer. There are two possible primary outcomes to a design attempt: The designer was able to find a design that satisfied its requirements or, it was not.

If the designer was successful, this means that the Designer succeeded in its objective. It doesn't mean that you are finished with the design of the girder. You must next run a comprehensive check to insure that the girder satisfies all specification provisions, and is optimized based on your own design criteria (minimum weight, cost, etc...). It is very likely that all criteria will not pass. The Details Report and Specification Check Report contain comprehensive check information.

If the designer was not successful, this means that the design algorithm failed to find a design to meet its objectives. This does not necessarily mean that the current girder cannot be made to pass all checks. However, it does mean that you will have to manually change the design parameters in order to create a design that passes all provisions.

It is also possible that the girder shape that you have selected simply cannot reach the span for your structure. In this case, you will need to consider changing the span length, adjusting the girder spacing, or selecting a larger girder.

Refer to Chapter 5 in the PGSuper User's Manual for more information.

Update the Current Girder Parameters with the Proposed Design?

After reviewing the design attempt, select "Reject the Design" if you want to keep the original (current) girder parameters, or "Accept the Design" to update the girder with PGSuper's design.

Note that even if the designer was unsuccessful, it will leave its last guess as an option to update the design parameters. You may want to use this as a starting point for your manual changes.
