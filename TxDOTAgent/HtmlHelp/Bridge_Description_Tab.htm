The General Information group contains textual data to contained in TOGA reports. None of this data affects the analysis.

Comments

Design Information

This group contains bridge-level design information

Span No

Span where the beam is located. (textual information only)

Beam No

Beam number. Generally numbered from left to right facing up-station. (textual information only)

Beam Type

Type of beam in question. Note that changing beam type will reset the strand data on the Girder Input tab to Standard Strand Fill with zero strands.

Beam Spacing

CL to CL spacing of beams.

Span Length

Length of span as measured between bearing centerlines. Note that the overall girder length is approximate and may not reflect the plan or as-fabricated girder length.

Slab Thickness

Equivalent slab thickness used to compute composite section properties and weight of slab. Slab density is assumed to be 150 pcf.

Relative Humidity

in % at bridge location

LLDF (Moment)

Live load distribution factor for moment

LLDF (Shear)

Live load distribution factor for shear

Material Properties

This group contains bridge-level material properties.

Ec, Slab

Modulus of elasticity of slab concrete

Ec, Beam

Modulus of elasticity of beam concrete

f'c, Slab

Final concrete strength of slab concrete

Design Data

This group contains the results from the analysis of the original per-plan beam design.

*ft, Design Compressive Stress, Top CL

Beam top fiber stress at centerline, due to total external load. (Service I, Final Stage)

*fb, Design Tensile Stress, Bottom CL

Beam bottom fiber stress at centerline, due to total external load. A negative sign {-} must be entered for tension. (Service III, Final Stage)

Mu, Required Ultimate Moment

Ultimate moment required

* Note: Tension is negative

Optional Uniform Design Loads

This group contains equivalent uniform design loads to be applied to the girder in the associated stages/load combinations.

w non-comp, dc

Uniform dead load, in addition to weight of beam and slab, applied to non-composite section in DC load case. (e.g. diaphragms).

w comp, dc

Uniform dead load, applied to composite section in DC load case (e.g., barrier)

w comp, dw

Uniform dead load, applied to composite section in DW load case (e.g., overlay)
